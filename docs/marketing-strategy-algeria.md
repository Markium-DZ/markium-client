# Markium — Marketing Strategy for the Algerian Market

## Market Context

**Algeria's e-commerce landscape:**
- ~45M population, 70%+ internet penetration, mostly mobile (Android dominant)
- E-commerce is early-stage but growing fast — most merchants are solo operators or small teams
- Facebook and TikTok are the primary sales channels (not Google Shopping or Amazon)
- Cash on Delivery (COD) is 95%+ of transactions — no credit card culture yet
- Delivery infrastructure is fragmented — dozens of local companies, no single dominant player
- Merchants communicate with customers via WhatsApp, not email
- Trust is the #1 barrier: customers don't trust online stores, merchants don't trust platforms
- Darija (Algerian Arabic) is the daily language; French for business; formal Arabic rarely used

**Competitor landscape:**
- Shopify/WooCommerce exist but are not localized for Algeria (no COD, no local delivery, no Darija)
- Local competitors are mostly basic store builders with no analytics
- No platform currently offers profitability tracking or CAPI management for Algerian merchants

## Positioning

### One-liner
**"Markium — the only e-commerce platform that speaks Algerian and shows you where your money goes."**

### Positioning statement
Markium is for Algerian merchants who sell through Facebook and TikTok ads. Unlike generic platforms, Markium is built for the Algerian market — COD payments, local delivery companies, Darija interface, and built-in tools to track exactly which ads make money and which don't.

### Key differentiators to push
1. **Darija-first** — the interface speaks like you do, not formal Arabic or English
2. **Built-in profitability** — know your profit per product, per campaign, per unit
3. **Pixel setup in 30 seconds** — Facebook/TikTok pixel + CAPI without a developer
4. **Customer tracking intelligence** — know if your customer saw their tracking page or not
5. **Made for COD** — the entire flow assumes cash on delivery, not credit cards

## Target Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Facebook/TikTok Ad Merchants (Primary — 70% of effort)
- **Who**: Solo operators or 2-3 person teams running product stores via Facebook/TikTok ads
- **Size**: Estimated 10,000-30,000 active merchants in Algeria
- **Pain**: They spend on ads but have no idea which campaigns are profitable. They use spreadsheets or nothing.
- **Hook**: "You spent 50,000 DA on ads last month. Do you know how much profit you actually made?"
- **Channel**: Facebook groups, TikTok, Instagram reels

### Segment 2: Growing Merchants (Secondary — 20% of effort)
- **Who**: Merchants doing 50-200+ orders/day, often with a small team
- **Pain**: They need delivery analytics, return rate tracking, and campaign ROI to scale
- **Hook**: "You're doing 100 orders/day. Which wilaya has the highest return rate? Which product has the best margin?"
- **Channel**: Direct outreach, WhatsApp communities, referrals

### Segment 3: Beginners Starting Their First Store (10% of effort)
- **Who**: Young Algerians (18-30) who want to start e-commerce
- **Pain**: Don't know where to start, intimidated by tech
- **Hook**: "Create your store in 5 minutes — in Darija, with everything you need"
- **Channel**: YouTube tutorials, TikTok educational content

## Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)

### Goal: Build credibility and seed content

#### 1.1 — Social Media Presence
- **Facebook Page** — primary channel, post in Darija
  - Content mix: 40% educational, 30% product features, 20% merchant success stories, 10% behind-the-scenes
  - Post frequency: 1x/day minimum
  - Format: short video reels (30-60s) > carousel > static image > text

- **TikTok Account** — reach younger merchants
  - Short-form tutorials: "How to set up Facebook Pixel in 30 seconds"
  - Before/after: "This merchant didn't know his profit margin. Now he does."
  - Trend-jacking with e-commerce angle

- **Instagram** — visual portfolio of merchant stores and platform screenshots
  - Reels (repurpose TikTok content)
  - Stories: daily tips, polls, Q&A

- **YouTube** — long-form tutorials and walkthroughs
  - "Complete Markium Setup Tutorial" (Darija)
  - "How to Track Your Ad Profitability"
  - "Understanding Your Analytics Dashboard"

#### 1.2 — Facebook Group: "Markium Community" / "مجتمع ماركيوم"
- Private group for Markium users + prospective merchants
- Daily value: tips, Q&A, merchant showcases
- Admin posts conversation starters: "What's your biggest challenge this week?"
- This becomes the #1 feedback loop and word-of-mouth engine

#### 1.3 — WhatsApp Broadcast List
- Collect numbers from signups and group members
- Weekly tips + feature announcements
- Personal touch — messages feel like they come from a person, not a brand

## Phase 2: Acquisition (Month 2-4)

### Goal: Get first 500 active merchants

#### 2.1 — Content Marketing (Organic)

**Educational content series (Darija):**

| Series | Format | Platform | Frequency |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|
| "Pixel w CAPI" | 60s reels | FB + TikTok + IG | 2x/week |
| "Fhemha b Darija" (Understand it in Darija) | Carousel | Facebook + IG | 2x/week |
| "Chhal Rbahti?" (How much did you profit?) | Short video | TikTok + Reels | 2x/week |
| "Store Setup" | YouTube tutorial | YouTube | 1x/week |
| "Merchant Spotlight" | Interview/story | FB Group + Page | 1x/week |

**Content pillars:**

1. **"You're losing money and you don't know it"** — profitability awareness
   - "You sold 200 orders but your margin is 3%. Here's how to find out."
   - "Your best-selling product might be your least profitable one."
   - "This merchant discovered 40% of his ad spend was wasted. Here's how."

2. **"Your pixels are broken"** — CAPI/tracking education
   - "Facebook is charging you more because your pixel isn't sending data properly."
   - "What is CAPI and why Meta keeps telling you to set it up?"
   - "Set up your pixel in 30 seconds — no developer needed."

3. **"Know your customer"** — tracking analytics
   - "Your customer checked their tracking 5 times. They want their order."
   - "The customer who never checks tracking? They might refuse delivery."
   - "This merchant reduced returns by 20% using tracking data."

4. **"Algerian e-commerce, Algerian tools"** — identity/pride angle
   - "Why Shopify doesn't work for Algeria"
   - "Built for COD. Built for your wilayas. Built in Darija."
   - Behind-the-scenes of building Markium

#### 2.2 — Paid Acquisition

**Facebook Ads (primary paid channel):**
- **Budget**: Start small (30,000-50,000 DA/month), scale what works
- **Targeting**: Interest in "e-commerce", "dropshipping", "Shopify", "online business" + Algeria
- **Lookalike**: Build from signup list once 100+ signups
- **Ad formats**:
  - Video testimonials from early merchants (Darija)
  - Screen recordings of the dashboard ("Look at this profit dashboard")
  - Comparison: "Your spreadsheet vs Markium" side-by-side
- **Landing page**: Simple, Darija-first, one CTA: "Create your free store"

**TikTok Ads:**
- Same creative approach, native TikTok style (not polished — raw, authentic)
- Target: 18-35, Algeria, interest in business/e-commerce

#### 2.3 — Partnerships & Influencers

**Micro-influencers (e-commerce educators):**
- Find 10-15 Algerian e-commerce educators on YouTube/TikTok/Facebook
- Offer: free Pro/Business plan + commission per signup (affiliate program)
- They create "How I use Markium" content for their audience
- Budget: product access + 500-1000 DA per paid signup

**Delivery company partnerships:**
- Partner with 3-5 major Algerian delivery companies
- Co-marketing: "Recommended by [delivery company]" badge
- Cross-promotion: delivery company mentions Markium to their merchants

**Facebook Group infiltration (ethical):**
- Identify top 20 Algerian e-commerce Facebook groups
- Become a helpful member first — answer questions, share tips
- Occasionally mention Markium when genuinely relevant
- Never spam — build reputation then leverage it

## Phase 3: Activation & Retention (Month 3-6)

### Goal: Convert signups to active users, reduce churn

#### 3.1 — Onboarding Flow
- **Day 0**: Welcome WhatsApp message (personal, in Darija)
- **Day 1**: "Add your first product" push notification / SMS
- **Day 2**: "Set up your Facebook Pixel" — with 30-second video tutorial link
- **Day 3**: "Your first order? Share the tracking link with your customer via WhatsApp"
- **Day 7**: "Here's your first weekly analytics report" — even if it's zeros, show them the dashboard
- **Day 14**: "You've had X visitors this week. Want to see where they came from? Upgrade to Pro."

#### 3.2 — The "Aha Moment" Map
Each feature has an aha moment — the instant a merchant sees value:

| Feature | Aha Moment | Trigger |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Analytics | First time seeing visitor count > 0 | First store visit |
| Profitability | Seeing actual profit margin (often shockingly low) | First cost entry |
| Pixel/CAPI | Seeing "Configured" badge + events flowing | First pixel setup |
| Tracking | Customer views the tracking page | First shared tracking URL |
| Tracking Analytics | Seeing "customer viewed while order was shipped" | First tracking page view |

**Strategy**: Push merchants toward each aha moment as fast as possible. The onboarding flow is designed around this.

#### 3.3 — Upgrade Triggers (Free → Pro → Business)
- **Blurred analytics** — merchants see locked charts, get curious, upgrade
- **Weekly email/WhatsApp**: "You had 340 visitors this week. Want to see your conversion rate? Upgrade to Pro."
- **Milestone triggers**: "You've passed 100 orders! Time to see which wilayas perform best → Upgrade to Business"
- **Profitability teaser**: Show total revenue on free plan, but profit/margin requires Business

#### 3.4 — Retention Tactics
- **Weekly analytics digest** via WhatsApp: "This week: 156 visitors, 12 orders, top product: X"
- **Monthly "Merchant Report"**: PDF or WhatsApp summary of key metrics
- **Feature drip**: Don't show everything at once. Reveal new features over time ("Did you know you can track your campaign ROI?")
- **Community engagement**: Active Facebook group creates switching cost

## Phase 4: Growth & Scale (Month 6-12)

### Goal: 2,000+ active merchants, sustainable revenue

#### 4.1 — Referral Program
- "Invite a merchant, get 1 month Pro free"
- Referral link trackable in the dashboard
- Top referrers get "Markium Ambassador" badge + Business plan free
- Leaderboard in the Facebook group

#### 4.2 — Case Studies & Social Proof
- **Video case studies** (2-3 min, Darija):
  - "How [merchant name] went from losing money to 25% profit margin"
  - "This merchant saves 2 hours/day with Markium analytics"
  - Before/after screenshots (with permission)
- **Revenue milestones**: "Markium merchants have processed X million DA in orders"
- **Testimonial wall** on the website

#### 4.3 — Events & Webinars
- **Monthly webinar**: "E-commerce Masterclass" — free, in Darija
  - Topics: pixel optimization, profitability, delivery management, ad strategy
  - Hosted on Facebook Live or Zoom
  - Recorded and repurposed as YouTube content
- **In-person meetups** in Algiers, Oran, Constantine (quarterly)
  - Small gatherings (20-50 merchants)
  - Networking + Markium demo + Q&A
  - Creates strong community bonds

#### 4.4 — Content Flywheel
By this point, the content machine should be self-sustaining:
- Merchants create their own content about Markium (UGC)
- Affiliates produce tutorials
- Facebook group generates daily organic discussion
- Case studies feed ad creative
- Webinar recordings become YouTube library

## Key Metrics to Track

### Acquisition
- Signups per week (target: 50+ by month 3)
- Cost per signup (target: < 200 DA)
- Signup-to-active conversion rate (target: 40%+)
- Channel attribution (which channel drives most signups)

### Activation
- Time to first product added (target: < 24h)
- Pixel setup rate within first week (target: 30%+)
- First order within first 2 weeks (target: 25%+)

### Revenue
- Free-to-Pro conversion rate (target: 15-20%)
- Pro-to-Business conversion rate (target: 10-15%)
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Average revenue per user (ARPU)
- Churn rate (target: < 5% monthly)

### Engagement
- Weekly active users (WAU)
- Facebook group engagement rate
- Feature adoption rates (analytics views, profitability usage, pixel setup)

## Messaging Playbook

### Core messages (rotate across all channels)

**Pain → Solution format (Darija mindset):**

1. "تخسر فلوس على الاعلانات وما تعرفش وين راهم يروحو؟ Markium يوريك الربح الحقيقي ديالك."
   *(You're losing money on ads and don't know where it goes? Markium shows you your real profit.)*

2. "بيكسل فيسبوك ديالك معطّل ولا ما ركبتوش؟ ركبّو في 30 ثانية بلا ما تحتاج developer."
   *(Your Facebook pixel is broken or not set up? Install it in 30 seconds without needing a developer.)*

3. "الزبون تاعك شاف الترقب 5 مرات — يبغي la commande تاعو. وإذا ما شافوش؟ يقدر يرفضها."
   *(Your customer checked tracking 5 times — they want their order. And if they didn't check? They might refuse it.)*

4. "كاين منتوج عندك يبيع بزاف بصح ما يدخلكش فلوس. Markium يقولك واش هو."
   *(You have a product that sells a lot but doesn't make money. Markium tells you which one.)*

   *(First e-commerce platform that speaks Darija. Built for COD, for wilayas, and for the Algerian merchant.)*
